Newport

City's population

  1. As of July 1, 2024 the population estimate is 24,482.

    2. Year city was incorporated

  2. Newport, Rhode Island, was first incorporated as a city on June 1, 1784. Although founded earlier in 1639, the city re-adopted a town form of government in 1787 before being re-incorporated as a city in 1853.

    3. Region located

  3. Newport, RI, is located on the southern tip of Aquidneck Island within Narragansett Bay. Situated in the southeastern part of the state, this coastal city serves as the seat of Newport County. It is approximately 33 miles southeast of Providence, 74 miles south of Boston, and 180 miles from New York City.

Classification of the city

  • Newport, RI, is best described as a dense suburban or small coastal city. While it serves as a historic, tourist-driven, and active harbor town, its high population density of over 3,200 people per square mile fits an urban-suburban profile rather than a rural one.
